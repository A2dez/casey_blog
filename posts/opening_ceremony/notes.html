Mood Music

The opening scene of the godfather pulls together the whole community while act 2 starts looking at his actions and interactions. (No spoilers please - it’s been a decade or two since I last watched it)

The equivalent scene in Dungarvan is the Opening Ceremony of the factory in 1937. All the characters are there. The Caseys. Dickens. Kennedy O’Brien. The government, most notably Lemass. I hope Monsignor Richard was there - he was certainly present at early board meetings. In the cortege behind are the workers and the wider town community. It’s a beautiful scene and from the off, it’s where I’ve wanted to anchor the book. Similar moments occur for funerals of Michael J, Catherine Casey and Austie. Other group scenes include the Christmas Parties and, coming in the other direction, the workers’ strike in maybe 1948, where they demanded better conditions.           Replying to the toast, Mr Lemass said thay they had gathered from the newspaper reports that he frequeently had occasion to attend functions like that, and for one holding the position fo Minister for Industry and Commerce it was always a pleasure to be present at such function, but the pleasure was not entirely an unmixed one, because as time passed it became increasingly difficult to say something original. This was the first occasion he was able to visit Dungarvan for a number of years, and he was very glad that the occasion was one associated with the establishment of an industry in the town, [with characteristic vision, he continued] an industry that would not merely be of considerable benefit to the town at present, but offers considerable prospects of development for the future, enabling it to extend its roll of its employees, enlarge the sum which is distributed in weekly wages, and thus extending the trade of the town, and help to add to its presperity, and promote the general business activities of the town.

        12. If there is one reason more than another why I am please to be associated with this enterprise, he said, is because in the best and truest sense of the term this is a local enterprise.
